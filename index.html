// Capabilities

Built for systems
that must run.

Fortis specializes in integrating and modernizing control systems for mission-critical facilities. Industrial-grade thinking applied to commercial and institutional infrastructure.

01 / Power & Energy

Power Monitoring & Energy Systems

Real-time visibility into electrical distribution, demand patterns, power quality, and energy performance. Integration with switchgear, panels, and utility metering for facility-wide intelligence.

02 / Backup Power

Generator & ATS Automation

Reliable backup power monitoring including auto-test sequencing, runtime logging, fuel tracking, and ATS failover analytics. NFPA 110 compliant. Critical for hurricane-prone South Florida facilities.

03 / Water Infrastructure

Pump Station Controls

Lead-lag sequencing, redundancy logic, VFD integration, and remote monitoring for stormwater, lift stations, and cooling infrastructure. Built for reliability when failure isn't an option.

04 / Modernization

PLC Modernization & Integration

Upgrade legacy control systems with modern Schneider Modicon PLCs, BACnet integration, and structured logic design. Bring old infrastructure into the current decade without ripping it out.

05 / Visibility

SCADA & Remote Monitoring

24/7 visibility into your critical systems through web-based dashboards, intelligent alarming, and automated reporting. Standardized monitoring protocols across all your facilities.

// About the Founder

Valdeci Godoy

Fortis Control Systems was founded by Valdeci Godoy, an engineer with a background spanning industrial automation, aerospace systems, and infrastructure controls.

His career began in industrial automation with Toyota, where he developed expertise in PLC programming, SCADA systems, and robotics integration. He later moved into aerospace, specializing in fuel tank ignition mitigation systems and serving as subject matter expert across multiple aircraft platforms, including the Boeing 737NG and Airbus A320/A321.

Fortis brings that industrial and aerospace discipline to commercial and institutional infrastructure — where reliability, documentation, and structured engineering matter most.
